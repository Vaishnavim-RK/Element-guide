🔬 The Periodic Table of Elements

The periodic table is a systematic arrangement of all known chemical elements based on their atomic properties. It helps scientists and students understand the relationship between different elements.

In 1869, the Russian chemist Dmitri Mendeleev proposed the first successful periodic table. He arranged elements in increasing order of atomic mass and noticed that elements with similar properties appeared at regular intervals – this is the Periodic Law.

Later, Henry Moseley improved the table by arranging elements according to their atomic number, which is the modern basis used today.

The table is divided into periods (rows) and groups (columns). Elements in the same group share similar chemical behavior.
